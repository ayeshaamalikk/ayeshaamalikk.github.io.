Research

From January 2023 to August 2024, I conducted HCI research at the eXtended Reality and Training Lab at UCF under the guidance of Dr. Ryan P McMahan. When Dr. McMahan moved to Virginia Tech in August 2024, I continued working with him remotely through present day (December 2025). We are studiyng cognitive underload in human-robot interaction settings using virtual reality.

In September 2024, I simultaneously joined the Computational Biology Lab at UCF, advised by Dr. Wei Zhang, where I have been working on machine learning approaches for cell deconvolution and multimodal biological data integration.

Benchmarking Cell Deconvolution Algorithms
Advised by Dr. Wei Zhang | Summer 2024

Conducted comprehensive benchmarking of ML-based cell deconvolution methods for single-cell RNA sequencing analysis. Award #2246796 focused on developing computational methods for integrating multi-dimensional biological data.

  • Preprocessed and annotated dataset of 45,000 gene expression samples
  • Implemented and evaluated 8 different cell deconvolution algorithms (TAPE, DISSECT, CIBERSORTx, SCADEN, ScpDeconv, BayesPrism)
  • Performed statistical analysis comparing predictions with ground truth
  • Fine-tuned parameters to optimize accuracy across all algorithms

VR Motor Assessment for Autism Spectrum Disorder
Advised by Dr. Ting Liu | Summer 2024

Developed a VR tool in Unity to assess motor skills of children with autism, recreating the MABC-2 motor assessment in virtual reality. Award #2150135.

  • Built Unity environment and designed experiment protocol
  • Processed resulting data and tested various ML supervised regression models
  • Determined most influential components of movement on motor skill proficiency
  • Pivoted study design when IRB approval was rescinded, proposing alternative validation study using VR-based performance metrics against real-world physics

Cognitive Underload in Human-Robot Interaction
Advised by Dr. Ryan McMahan | Aug 2024 - Present

Examining how different cue modalities affect attention and cognitive underload during repetitive tasks in VR. This study intentionally manipulates salience and augmentation to test how small changes in environment design can improve focus, teamwork, and engagement in human-robot interaction settings.

  • Built Unity environment and designed experiment protocol
  • Conducted extensive literature reviews on cognitive load theory
  • Wrote IRB protocol and managed approval process
  • Created Blender animations for robot interactions
  • Trained PhD student to run participants, creating comprehensive documentation
  • Currently writing manuscript for publication
Status:
Study in progress, manuscript in preparation
